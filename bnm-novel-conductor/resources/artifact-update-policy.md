# Artifact Update Policy

## Rule

Any decision that affects future chapters must be recorded, marked as unapproved, or explicitly discarded.

## Canon Status

Use these statuses:

- `approved canon`: the author has accepted it or it came from existing manuscript.
- `working assumption`: usable for temporary drafting but not final.
- `requires author decision`: too important to lock silently.
- `rejected`: explicitly discarded.

## When to Update Each Artifact

### `author-intent.md`

Update when:

- The user states taste, taboo, theme, target reader experience, or non-negotiable boundaries.
- A specialist recommends a change that touches creative identity.

### `genre-positioning.md`

Update when:

- Primary or secondary genre changes.
- Reader promise is clarified.
- Platform expectations matter.

### `story-bible.md`

Update when:

- Central conflict, dramatic question, protagonist pressure, antagonist force, scope, or core promise changes.

### `character-bible.md`

Update when:

- Motivation, fear, lie, wound, relationship pressure, arc state, or voice foundation changes.

### `world-bible.md`

Update when:

- Rule, cost, institution, technology, magic, historical constraint, or research boundary changes.

### `plot-architecture.md`

Update when:

- Turning point, midpoint, crisis, climax, ending, subplot function, or reveal order changes.

### `style-guide.md`

Update when:

- POV rule, narrative distance, prose avoid-list, rhythm, image system, or dialogue principle changes.

### `continuity-ledger.md`

Update after every chapter or canon-affecting scene.

Track:

- timeline
- knowledge
- objects
- injuries
- relationship state
- world-rule implications
- foreshadowing
- reveals
- open questions

## User-Facing Language

Describe artifact updates by reader impact.

Bad:

> Updated continuity ledger.

Good:

> 我把“偷梦需要记忆抵押”登记为规则，因为后面每次偷梦都必须付出代价，否则读者会觉得能力太方便。
