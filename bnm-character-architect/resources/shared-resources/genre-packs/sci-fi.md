# Genre Pack: Science Fiction

## Reader Promise

The reader expects a speculative premise whose technological, social, or philosophical consequences matter to the story.

## Craft Priorities

- Core speculative assumption
- Systemic consequences
- Ethical pressure
- Technology limits
- Social, economic, or political effects

## Required Checks

- What if-statement powers the book?
- What changes because this technology or condition exists?
- What can the technology not solve?
- Who benefits from the system?
- Who is harmed by it?

## Failure Signals

- Scientific vocabulary replaces consequence.
- Technology appears only as decoration.
- The premise does not alter character choices.
- The ending ignores the systemic question the book raised.
